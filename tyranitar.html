Tyranitar (Non-Shiny)

Tyranitar (Shiny)

Why this Pokemon?

Tyranitar is a physically defensive semi-wall usually having earthquake. I have only used Tyranitar in competitive play a few times but he is pretty good for things such as breaking sash and sturdy with his ability making him a viable pick for competitive. I feel as though the only reason I like Tyranitar is because it was one of my brother's favorite Pokemon, alongside Gengar, which in turn caused me to be an avid enjoyer of it. I do like the way the mon plays, as well as looks, but other than that it's a very niche Pokemon that is usually end game, which means by the time you get to it, you already have a squad layout that you are using. Although this last statement still stands, it dosen't mean this Pokemon cannot be a viable pick for competitve play. This mon is extremely useful for competitive play, as not only is it good at breaking sash and sturdy, but it can also let out powerful earthquake attacks. And with heavy duty boots, this Pokemon becomes immune to hazards such as spikes and toxic spikes, making incoming damage on switch useless. Although this mon is very powerful, it only uses that strength to make up for how slow it is. Overall, this Pokemon is very solid, it's able to take hits and deal good damage.

Information/Comp Usage

Typing: Rock/Dark

Ability: "Sand Stream"- When this Pokemon is sent out it causes a sandstorm to form (doesent do damage to steel, ground, and rock types.)

Move Set: The best moveset for Tyranitar is Stone edge, Crunch, Earthquake, and Fire Punch.

Prefered Held item: Heavy Duty Boots - Pokemon is immune to hazards upon entering the field.

Role: Bulky Physical attacker.
